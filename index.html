Who is Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam?

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was an extraordinary scientist and an inspiring leader who became the 11th President of India. His dedication to education, science, and the nation's development left a lasting legacy.

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to lead groundbreaking advancements in aerospace and nuclear technology. His birth anniversary is celebrated as World Students' Day, marking his passion for empowering the youth. Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931. His birth anniversary is celebrated as World Students Day. He was the President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was honored with several prestigious awards including the "Bharat Ratna", India's highest civilian honor in 1997. Achievements

  • In 1981, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India.
  • In 1990, he was awarded the Padma Vibhushan.
  • In 1997, he was awarded the Bharat Ratna.
  • In 1998, he was awarded the Veer Savarkar Award.
  • In 2000, Alvaras Research Centre, Chennai presented him with the Ramanujan Award.
  • In 2007, Britain awarded him the King Charles II Medal by the Royal Society.
  • In 2008, he received the Doctor of Engineering (Honoris Causa) from Nanyang Technical University, Singapore.
  • In 2015, the United Nations recognized his birthday as "World Students' Day".
